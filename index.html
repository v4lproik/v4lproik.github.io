Chronology

Career path, stage by stage

The sequence matters. Each move built on the previous one: security led to data infrastructure, infrastructure led to cryptographic systems, and that eventually led into crypto research, operations, and building a company around execution.

Stage 01

Offensive security for the French government

I entered the field early, working as a professional hacker at 20. That meant learning fast under real constraints: offensive security, attack surfaces, operational discipline, and what robust systems look like when someone is trying to break them.

01 Origin Security first, with real consequences and no patience for hand-wavy engineering.
Stage 02

Big data, streaming, and predictive market systems

I then moved into distributed data systems: Spark, Spark Streaming, complex data pipelines, and prediction engines. At Amadeus, that included work around airline slot resale, heterogeneous ingestion including VOIP streams, and automated bidding and resale logic driven by complex prediction models.

02 Scale High-throughput systems, noisy inputs, and decision-making under latency.
Stage 03

Cryptography, pentesting, and security platforms

From there I went deeper into security again, working with multiple governments on cryptography, pentesting, and sophisticated security platforms. The thread was always the same: build systems that stay coherent when the environment is hostile.

03 Assurance Security as system design, not a checklist bolted on after the fact.
Stage 04

Crypto research for node operators and networks

In crypto I worked with node operators and protocol-facing teams on research that tied software behavior back to infrastructure reality: single-slot finality, validator performance, and the effect of hardware, geography, and compute on networks operating at serious scale. I also run nodes myself from my own lab and bare-metal servers, which keeps the work grounded in operational reality rather than theory.

sol-validator-watcher
04 Research Protocol ideas only matter once they are tested against the network they claim to improve.
Stage 05

Founder mode: build the company, not just the code

Eventually the work stopped being only technical. I built my own company from zero to 20 employees and raised $13.4M. The challenge shifted from shipping systems myself to building the team, culture, and execution machine that could do it repeatedly. Even there, I stayed hands-on: close to architecture, close to product, and close to the people doing the work. The role became broader, but the operating style stayed the same: strong technical leadership, close support for the team, and high standards on execution.
